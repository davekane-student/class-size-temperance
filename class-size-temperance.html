Class Size: Temperance

The Question

The Tennessee STAR experiment (Mosteller 1995) randomly assigned more than 6,000 students in about 80 Tennessee schools to small classes (13–17 students), regular classes (22–25), or regular classes with a teacher’s aide, from kindergarten through third grade. Our data records each student’s kindergarten class-type assignment, race, years spent in small classes across grades K through 3, and fourth-grade score on the math computation portion of the Stanford Achievement Test.

Imagine you work for the Texas Department of Education. You want to understand the effect of small classes on student performance in Dallas. In Texas, there is data available like the data in the STAR project.

The Texas Department of Education asks: What is the average causal effect of assigning a Dallas student to a small class on their fourth-grade math score?
